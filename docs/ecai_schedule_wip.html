ECAI-2024 — Schedule

This is a draft schedule for the technical sessions at ECAI, excluding invited talks, panel sessions, demo sessions, and PAIS. This version of the schedule includes lots of extra management information, which you can ignore and which we will drop for the final version. For general information on the schedule, please refer to the ECAI website.

Please carefully read the notes below before contacting us. If you do need to get in touch, please use the following form, and do so no later than Monday, 9 September 2024 (end of day, AoE).

To the form

In exceptional cases, you can also reach us by emailing pc-chairs@ecai2024.eu, but we will prioritise requests from people using the form.

This schedule is tentative and we might still make small changes. For changes made after 30 September 2024, we will email everyone directly affected, but for changes made in September we won't usually do that.

Papers will get presented either orally or as posters. Each oral presentation will get a 15-minute slot (12-minute talk + questions). Each poster will get a 2-minute spotlight talk in the session indicated, with the poster itself being presented during the 90-minute poster session taking place at the end of the same day. Each 'Frontiers in AI' talk will get a 30-minute slot (25-minute talk + questions).

For most papers, the oral/poster decision has not yet been taken. We plan to do this by 16 September 2024 (sorry, one week later than originally announced) and then update this schedule accordingly. However:

  1. If you explicitly requested a poster slot or if you requested the use of a proxy, you definitely will get a poster slot (shown as 'RP' in the schedule, in the column after your paper number). If we made a mistake, please let us know through the form.
  2. There will be no poster session on Thursday, so all Thursday papers will get oral presentation slots.
  3. All journal-track papers will get oral presentation slots.

We believe that this schedule respects all scheduling constraints communicated to us by the official deadline in July. If we made a mistake, please let us know through the form. Please understand that we won't be able to accommodate new scheduling constraints.

We believe that this schedule does not require any author (or proxy) to be in two places at the same time. If we made a mistake, please let us know through the form.

FYI, most sessions are 90 minutes long (only the Wednesday morning session is 120 minutes long). For some slots there are fewer parallel sessions than for others. This is due to other events taking place in parallel (e.g., panel sessions).

FYI, we have used the bids submitted by PC members during the reviewing process to estimate similarities between papers and then grouped them into sessions accordingly. The scores shown reflect these similarities (and the #0 column shows how many papers in the same session are unrelated to the paper in question, according to this notion of similarity). We are fully aware of the fact that the resulting schedule is not perfect, which is unavoidable given the large number of constraints and the imperfections in the data it is based on.
